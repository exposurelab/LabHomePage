Environmental Health Assessment Laboratory

Research Direction

The research focuses of our laboratory are

  1. the temporal and spatial distributions and the source apportionment of organic aerosols in the air.
  2. the exposure and risk assessment of toxic chemicals in the air and food.

The current research topics are

  1. assessing persistent organic pollutants (POPs) such as polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs), and chlorinated pesticides in the aerosols;
  2. evaluating the size and temporal distributions of ionic species of aerosols;
  3. exploring the interaction of air pollutants and meteorological parameters and hopefully projecting the potential impact of climate change on public health;
  4. assessing the sources, levels, exposure pathways, and exposure factors fn the personal aerosol exposure in different micro-environments.

For example, we assessed residents’exposure to aerosols and PAHs during worshipping activities at home, in the temple and during Tachia Ma-Tsu Goddess parade and evaluated the contribution of potential exposure factors; we compared the commuters’exposure to aerosols and PAHs with different commuting modes (motorcycle, car, and subway); we also assessed the contribution of dust events and biomass burning on the air quality of Taiwan.

  ion chromatography (IC)

IC is used to analyze ionic species of aerosol such as sulfate, nitrate, ammonium, sodium, chloride, etc.

  gas chromatography (GC)

GC is connected to two different detectors: electron capture detector (ECD)and flame ionization detector (FID) to analyze polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs), polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs) and chlorinated pesticides

  GC mass spectrometry

Besides analyzing PAHs, PCBs, and chlorinated pesticides, GC/MS is used to analyze source tracers such as those emitted from vehicle and fragrances.

  liquid chromatography - triple-quadrupole mass spectrometry (LC/MS/MS)

LC/MS/MS is used to analyze soluble organics in the aerosols such as tracers from biomass burning and tire dusts and organic acids; it can also be used for analyze pharmaceuticals and active ingredients of personal care products in the water environments which may be harmful to biota in the waters.
